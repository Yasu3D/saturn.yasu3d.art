# Glossary
Some explanations for terms that are commonly used in this documentation.

#### Beat
A subdivision of a [Measure](#measure).

#### Beat Division
The number of Beats in a [Measure](#measure).

#### Beat Line Note
A thin, grey, ring that represents a [Beat](#beat).

#### Bonus Type
Special variations of [playable](#playable) [Notes](#note) that grant more clear rate, or more score.

#### Bookmark
A marker with a custom color and message.

#### Chain Note
A striped yellow [Note](#note) that is hit by touching or holding down within its area. No timing is required.

#### Chart
A collection of [Events](#event), [Notes](#note), and [Bookmarks](#bookmark) that defines the gameplay for a Song.

#### Content
A collective term to refer to [Charts](#chart), [Cosmetics](#cosmetic), and [Stage-Up Stages](#stage-up-stage)

#### Cosmetic
A piece of content that the player can choose in SATURN to customize their profile. 

#### Chart File
A file that saves data for a [Chart](#chart).

#### Division
See [Beat Division](#beat-division)

#### Edit Mode
A [Mode](#mode) in the Chart Editor. Edits an [object](#object) more specifically.

#### Event
An [object](#object) that changes the state of a [Chart](#chart) in some way, or triggers a special effect. (e.g. Tempo Change, Metre Change, Speed Change)

#### External File
A file that is referenced by [content](#content), usually an image or audio file.

#### Hitsound
Sound effects that play when certain objects reach the [Judgement Line](#judgement-line).

#### Hold Note
A long yellow/orange [Note](#note) that is hit by touching and holding down within its area at the correct time.

#### Jacket
The image of the song to display on the song select screen. Also known as "album artwork", "cover artwork", or "album cover".

#### Judgement Line
A bright magenta/purple ring that marks the "correct" time when to hit a [Note](#note).

#### Judgement Type
Special variations of [playable](#playable) [Notes](#note) that change how they are judged during gameplay.

#### Lane
A space that part of a [positionable](#positionable) [object](#object) can occupy. The [play area](#play-area) is divided into 60 lanes.

#### Lane Toggle
A type of [Note](#note) that changes the visibility of individual [lanes](#lane).

#### Measure
A collection of [Beats](#beat) or [Ticks](#tick). Same as a Measure in musical notation.  
Sometimes referred to as a "Bar".

#### Measure Line Note
A thin, white, ring that represents the beginning of a [Measure](#measure).

#### Mode
The state that the Chart Editor is in.
See [Object Mode](#object-mode) and [Edit Mode](#edit-mode).

#### Note
An [object](#object) in a [Chart](#chart) that is either judged in gameplay, or acts as a visual guide/decoration.

#### Object
A collective term for individual "parts" of a [Chart](#chart).

#### Object Mode
A [Mode](#mode) in the Chart Editor. Treats everything as a single [object](#object).

#### Operation
A single piece of work that can be undone/redone.

#### Playable
A [Note](#note) that is relevant to gameplay.

#### Play Area
The area where [Notes](#note) approach.

#### Position
The [lane](#lane) where the clockwise-most ("leftmost") part of a [positionable](#positionable) [Note](#note) lies.

#### Positionable
A [Note](#note) that has a [Position](#position) and [Size](#size).

#### Quantization
The act of aligning a rhythm or single timestamp to a fixed beat grid.

#### Size
The number of [Lanes](#lane) that a [Note](#note) occupies.

#### Slide Note
An orange or green [Note](#note) that is hit by flicking sideways within its area at the correct time.

#### Snap Note
A red or blue [Note](#note) that is hit by flicking forward or back within its area at the correct time.

#### Stage
See [Stage-Up Stage](#stage-up-stage)

#### Stage-Up
A special game-mode where three fixed songs are played in succession, with limited health. 
Every mistake subtracts health, and if it depletes, the game is over.

#### Stage-Up Stage
A set of three songs to play in [Stage-Up](#stage-up) mode.

#### Sweep Animation
A sweeping animation that plays when a [Lane Toggle Note](#lane-toggle) reaches the [Judgement Line](#judgement-line).

#### Sweep Direction
The direction the [sweep animation](#sweep-animation) of a [Lane Toggle Note](#lane-toggle) goes in.

#### Sync Note
A thin blue [Note](#note) that connects two simultaneous Notes.

#### Tick
1/1920th of a [Measure](#measure)

#### Timestamp
The time an [object](#object) occupies in a [Chart](#chart).

#### Touch Note
A magenta [Note](#note) that is hit by touching within its area at the correct time.

#### Tunnel
See [Play Area](#play-area).
